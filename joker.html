The Joker

No one really knows who the Joker is, nor his true origin story. But I believe he was an unsuccessful amateur comedian. After a really bad day and the death of his pregnant wife, he became the petty criminal, Red Hood. Then, during a fight, I accidentally threw him into a pit of acid and it completely dismorphed his face. He then became insane and adopted his new identity: the Joker.

Of course I know his identity... Or should I say, his three identities! After sitting on a chair, I saw it. Well, it didn't tell me exactly who the Joker was, but it told me how many they were... It caught me so off guard I could only say "No, that's not possible"!

Can't believe this? Check this out then: Who Are 3 different Jokers According To Mobius Chair? or this The Three Joker Theory? - Know Your Universe | Comicstorian.

The Criminal Mastermind

He was some sort of a genius that used inventive and creative gadgets to rob banks, had this love for weapons and money and wouldn't mind killing people. A weird looking gangster. He was violent and he did it all for the money. In fact, he retired after becoming rich! And he came back when he realized all that money was fake. Ha ha ha! The prankster got pranked!

He was sadistic, brutal, he made announces on the radio (and now on television) about the things he was about to do, and then HE ACTUALLY DID THEM! A true mass murderer!

This version of the Joker was the first one to exist. It belonged to the Golden Era (1938-1950).

The Goofy Prankster

Suddenly everything calmed down. The Joker was robbing banks, using his creative gadgets, but was no longer killing people. He became much less violent and much more of a goofball. It was a bit difficult to take him seriously. He was just mildly annoying honestly!

It was getting ridiculous! We were simply playing pranks on each other! Sure, some seemed more dangerous than others, but no one really got severely hurt by those.

This version belongs to the calm Silver Age (1956 - 1970).

The Maniac Psychopath

So now the Joker is back on killing people, yey! But now it's not because he's a Criminal Mastermind. This is about mental instability. He was starting to play mind tricks and go on killing sprees ... Being all "philosophical" and coming with this mindset of nothing really matters and "this is all a joke".

With this huge levels of madness and insanity, the Joker became really dangerous! He kept saying that he only existed because of me! So, is it my fault that you're insane? Hell, the Joker even took his face off and then stapled it back like a mask only TO MAKE A POINT! Of course he eventually came "back to normal" because of the Universe Shenanigans and stuff...

This last version began on the Bronze Age (1970-1985) and only got worse in the Modern Age (1985 - Up to nowadays).

Everything is a joke for Joker!! Nothing really matters and so the consequences of our actions are meaningless. So he has no remorse in killing people. It doesn't matter. And if we think about it, if in this universe we can get away with killing characters who end up coming back, either by being resurrected by some pit or crystal, or by some shenanigan, that happens...

Then the Joker is right: nothing really matters in the Universe.
